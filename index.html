Introduction

For this project my corpus is the composer Ludovico Einaudi and the albums he has made. When I was little, my mom used to play music from Ludovico Einaudi on the piano. I always loved to listen to them and when I started to play the piano myself I also wanted to play these pieces. I also listen to the recordings of Einaudis music that are on his spotify account. I listen to them when I am happy, sad, studying, traveling, basically any and every time. You could say that Einaudi is one of my favorite componists. Einaudi started composing in the 80s and hasnt stopped ever since. It is safe to say that when someone is doing something for so long, they evolve and learn allong the ride. Einaudi made various albums all of which belong in the genre minimalism. I wonder if and what the difference is between these albums. Is his style changed? Are there subgenres that can be assigned to the various albums? I wil be using all of the albums and put them in a playlist together. If there are albums that have a normal version and a deluxe version, I wil be using the deluxe version. I wont look at remixes or live albums. It is very typical for Einaudi to create themes that are present through the musical piece. He also often adds strings to his pieces. It is atypical for him to use singing in his pieces, but it has been added for some pieces.

After the feedback on my corpus, I have decided to look only at the Seven days walking albums which consists of 7 albums for each day of the week. Einaudi released these albums in 2019. I think is would be very interesting to see the differences between these albums, because they are from the same year and have the same theme, but are yet different from each other.

Self-similarity matrices Birdsong Day 2

The timbre based self-similarity matrice shows the change in the music from around 80 second mark; the music becomes higher and faster. After that it turns back blue. The music also changes again at that point to lower notes and calmer tempo. The chroma-based self-similarity matrice also show these changes in regarding to the pattern. the colors dont give away much about the pitches, but you see a different pattern on the 100 second mark. At the end of this piece, the music theme is different, as you can see in the more yellow in the graph. At the 300 second mark, it goes back to the beginning theme; there the pattern is the same as the pattern in the beginning of the graph. This phenomenon isnt noticable in the timbre-based graph.

Heatmap - Seven Days Walking

The heatmap has the same problem as the dendrogram, because of the big amount of music pieces that I fitted in the graph, but the graph should be active, so with zooming in, I can look at all the details. In the Seven days walking albums is no singing, so it doesnt come as a surprise that the acousticness and instrumentalness of all the pieces is very yellow. The speechiness, liveness and valence on the other hand are mostly blue. Only the Golden Butterflies Var. 2 - Day 2 is yellow in liveness (it has a value of 1) and only the Low Mist Var. 1 - Day 6 is yellow in valence (also a value of 1). A few of the Cold Wind pieces are more green/yellow in speechiness, but they dont contain actual speech. Based on the chordograms I saw that Birdsong - Day 2 mostly C major and A minor was and Birdsong - Day 7 mostly G minor was. The heatmap doesnt support the assumption that Birdsong - Day 2 is C major or A minor, because the D sharp/E flat note is very bright yellow (value of 0,93) and this note is not in the C major of A minor scale. The A and C note themselves are not dark blue, but also way to green to be represented enough in the music piece that would lead to believe the piece is in C major or A minor. On the other hand, Birdsong - Day 7 could be in G minor, according to the heatmap. G minor has B flat and E flat in its scale and these two notes are very green and the note E for example is very dark blue. The note A flat is also very green, so it might be possible that G minor is not the mode that the piece is in.

Conclusion

The seven albums from Seven Days Walking are very diverse, but also have a lot of similarities. When I listen to the albums, I hear the similarities and the differences, but when I look at the graphs on certain points, the pieces seem to be more different than I am able to hear (for example the timbre-based graphs). Some graphs also dont support each other. I do think that the heatmap gave me the most insight, because I had all the pieces in one graph with a lot of variables. And as I could see were on a lot of variables the pieces the same (like the almost completely yellow instrumentalness) and also that for example the duration was very different in every piece. In conclusion, the pieces are very similar in the way they are being heard (acousticness, lack of speechiness etc.), but they are very different in for example the mode they are in. All in all I think that the difference once you know they are there and what the difference are (obvious differences like if there is a violin playing yes or no excluded), you can definitely hear them, but otherwise most pieces sound very similar.
